# PhD Student | Dept. of Biological Science | Vanderbilt University

I am a molecular and computational biologist currently pursuing my PhD in the Plate Lab at Vanderbilt University. My research investigates how the structural integrity of the ribosome orchestrates co-translational quality control. By using fast-misfolding proteins (like P67L-CFTR) as translational biosensors, I study how targeted ribosomal stress can remodel the global chaperome and act as a "biological substitute" for pharmacological therapeutics.

Beyond the bench, I am work to resolve the bioinformatic bottlenecks that limit complex multi-omics integration. I develop interactive, coding-free software platforms designed to make high-depth proteomics and translation dynamics accessible, reproducible, and easy to understand.

# Featured Bioinformatic Platforms

## PICK (Proteome Investigation and Characterization Kit)

An interactive, coding-free pipeline builder for multi-omics integration.

The Problem: Integrating DIA/TMT-DDA whole-cell proteomics (WCP-MS), affinity purification (AP-MS), and cross-linking (XL-MS) datasets often requires extensive coding expertise and disparate pipelines.

The Solution: PICK automates median normalization, missing-value imputation, and differential expression analysis. It features custom modules for direct mapping to the Proteostasis Network (PN) schema, allowing researchers to instantly generate publication-ready volcano plots, hierarchical heatmaps, pathway-level abundance boxplots, and clusterProfileR GO analysis. Best of all, it is all in one place with a steamlined savespace to never lose your progress, or try something new!

## PolyPlotR

A purpose-built quantification and data visualization tool for translation dynamics.

The Problem: Existing software struggles with the noisy baselines and complex integration required for precise polysome profile and Harringtonine (HTN) runoff analysis.

The Solution: PolyPlotR processes continuous A260 absorbance or any number of fluorescense traces (e.g., from TRIAX flow cells) with Savitzky-Golay smoothing and dynamic peak-to-peak thresholding options. It allows for the streamlined calculation of Area Under the Curve (AUC) for 40S, 60S, 80S, and polysome fractions. Native HTN runoff analysis is first in its field, and can be run in-unit or exported to your favorite plotting program. 

# Current Research Focus

Translation Dynamics: Decoupling global elongation velocity from structural subunit imbalance using Harringtonine runoff assays, Ribo-seq, and polysome profiling.

Co-Translational Proteostasis: Mapping how the physical periphery of the exit tunnel selectively recruits molecular chaperones (NAC/RAC) and regulates non-canonical translation initiation (eIF4G2).

Targeted Ribostress: Defining the intersection between the canonical 40S degradation alarm (RNF10/RIOK3) and targeted, pro-folding interactome remodeling.
